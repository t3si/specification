# The System Stewardship Standard (T3S)
### *A Unified Framework for Ontological System Governance*
**An Initiative of T3SI (The System Stewardship Standards Initiative)**

---

## 0.0 Foreword
The **System Stewardship Standard (T3S)** is a normative specification for the organization, indexing, and ontological governance of complex technical environments. It establishes a **Deterministic File-System Hierarchy** that functions as a "Reality Engine" for AI-augmented workflows. 

T3S is specifically engineered to solve the **"Accountability Vacuum"**—the structural tension in highly regulated and high-hazard industries (FinTech, Infrastructure, EHS) between high-level policy and low-level execution. By utilizing **Linked Data (JSON-LD)**, T3S ensures that system intent, architectural guardrails, and operational evidence are semantically anchored, preventing "Ontological Drift" and ensuring autonomous agents operate within human-defined **Markov Blankets**.

## 1.0 Stewardship Mandates (Objectives)
T3S is governed by five immutable objectives:

* **100.100 Objective A: Deterministic File-System Mapping (DFM).** Absolute predictability of artifact location via a multi-tier, 3-digit recursive indexing protocol (e.g., `130.110.000`).
* **100.200 Objective B: Semantic Stewardship.** Provision of a **JSON-LD based Knowledge Graph** bridging machine-readable manifests (000) and human-authored standards (100-900), ensuring agents interpret **Systemic Intent** with absolute fidelity.
* **100.300 Objective C: Digital Sovereignty & Verifiable AI.** Mandating structures that support local-first privacy, **Formal Verification Proofs**, and **ZKML (Zero-Knowledge Machine Learning)** circuits within a self-hosted context.
* **100.400 Objective D: ITIL-Symmetry.** Maintaining a direct link between industry-standard Service Management and physical repository architecture, from System Control (000) to Operations (500).
* **100.500 Objective E: Verifiable Insurability.** Developing a standardized **"Standard of Care"** that shifts governance from a **Statement of Belief into a Technical Boolean**. This ensures technical telemetry is transformed into forensic evidence, enabling the creation of pre-underwritten, risk-mitigated environments for autonomous agents.

## 2.0 Normative Scope & Constraints
Compliance with T3S requires adherence to the following structural mandates:

### 2.1 Structural Integrity (The Taxonomy)
* **The Markov Blanket (000 Block):** The **000 Block** serves as the system's **Ontological Boundary**. It contains the machine-readable JSON-LD manifests that define the "Laws of Physics" and safety guardrails for the environment.
* **The Observational Plane (100-900):** These blocks constitute the human-interface layer, providing the narrative and evidence required for a **System Steward** to monitor, audit, and calibrate the AI’s field of intent.
* **Naming Discipline:** All entities MUST utilize the **Anchor and Leaf Protocol** defined in `130.000`. Filenames must lead with their full recursive index (e.g., `XXX.XXX.000`).

### 2.2 Technical Scope
* **In-Scope: In-Sovereign Governance:** Formalization of the **Standard of Care (120.000)** into core logic gates. This ensures the architecture acts as the primary enforcer of systemic intent, rather than relying on external, subjective policy documents.
* **In-Scope: Agentic Grounding (000 Block):** Provision of machine-readable manifests and persona constraints that establish the **Contextual Truth** for autonomous agents. This prevents "hallucination" by strictly defining the agent's field of observational reality.
* **In-Scope: Automated Intent Validation (300 Block):** CI/CD pipelines configured as "Governance Gates." These verify technical states against normative policies before any structural persistence or deployment occurs.
* **In-Scope: Service Evidence & Asset Tracking (400 Block):** Management of **CMDB configurations** to maintain a verifiable topography of sovereign infrastructure and assets.
* **In-Scope: Forensic Persistence (500 Block):** Creation of an **Immutable Evidence Database (EDB)**. This capability transforms raw operational telemetry into verifiable forensic records, enabling the pre-underwritten risk mitigation required for verifiable insurability.

* **Out-of-Scope: Application Source Code.** T3S is a governance framework; functional source code should reside in sub-indexed modules or external repositories referenced via manifest hash.
* **Out-of-Scope: Unstructured Binary Data.** Managed via pointer/hash references within the 400 or 500 blocks to maintain repository performance and forensic integrity.
* **Out-of-Scope: Non-Prefixed Artifacts.** Any file lacking a valid T3S prefix is a violation of the standard and must be rejected by the governance gate.

### 2.3 The Semantic Anchor (JSON-LD Knowledge Graph)
Unlike traditional hierarchical structures that rely on path-based assumptions, T3S employs **Linked Data (JSON-LD)** within the 000-Block to transform the directory from a passive storage tree into an **Active Knowledge Graph**.

- **Contextual Linking:** Every artifact is semantically mapped to its governing policy (100-block) and its operational evidence (500-block). This creates a bidirectional link between _why_ a rule exists and _how_ it was executed.
    
- **Drift Prevention:** By anchoring files to a global ontology (ITIL, ISO, T3S), the standard ensures that **Systemic Intent** remains high-fidelity. As the underlying infrastructure or agentic models evolve, the semantic meaning of the governance remains locked to its original mandate.
    
- **Machine-Verifiable Reality:** This anchor allows automated auditors to perform graph-based queries across the repository, determining compliance not just by file presence, but by the logical relationship between intent, action, and evidence.

## 3.0 Core Repository Taxonomy
* **000 | System Control and Markov Blankets:** The "Source of Truth" for system axioms, agentic persona constraints, and the ontological boundaries of the environment.
* **100 | Governance and Strategy:** The "Human Intent" layer; housing the Portfolio, **Standard of Care (120.000)**, and the normative protocols for systemic stewardship.
* **200 | System Architecture and Design:** The "Logical Blueprint"; encompassing Ontological Schemas, Agentic Logic-Flows, and **Verifiable AI/ZKML Circuits**.
* **300 | Platform Engineering and Automation:** The "Enforcement Layer"; containing IaC, governance-as-code validation pipelines, and automated audit triggers.
* **400 | Infrastructure and Assets:** The "Physical Inventory"; including CMDB, verifiable network topographies, and sovereign hardware configurations.
* **500 | Technical Operations and Forensic Evidence:** The "Reality Record"; runbooks, incident management, and the **Evidence Database (EDB)** for verifiable insurability.
* **600 | Observability, Feedback and Learning:** The "Alignment Loop"; monitoring system health, model drift, and formalizing post-incident systemic evolution.
* **900 | Tools and Utilities:** The "Operational Support" layer; including the Global Lexicon, scaffolding templates, and the **990 Global Archive**.

## 4.0 The Alignment Loop (Evolution)
T3S is not a static repository but a recursive feedback engine. Through the 600-block, the system monitors **Model Drift** and **Operational Anomalies**, feeding forensic data from the 500-block back into the 100-block (Strategy). This creates a formal path for **Systemic Evolution** that remains under human stewardship, ensuring the Markov Blanket remains intact as the environment scales.
