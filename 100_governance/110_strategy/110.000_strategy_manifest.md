# 110 Strategy and Portfolio Manifest

## 1.0 Purpose (ITIL Strategy Alignment)
This manifest defines the **Service Strategy** and **Portfolio Management** for the T3S framework. It aligns technical evolution with the organizational objectives of the **System Stewardship Standards Initiative (T3SI)**, ensuring deterministic value creation for stewards, partners, and AI agents.

## 2.0 Strategic Objectives (The Beta Roadmap)
The T3SI portfolio is managed across three primary horizons:

### 2.1 Horizon 1: The Genesis Block (Current)
* **Goal:** Finalize the **v0.2.0-alpha** structural substrate.
* **Success Criteria:** 100% compliance with the **Anchor and Leaf Protocol** and the closure of the **Markov Blanket** via cryptographic signatures.

### 2.2 Horizon 2: The Beta Expansion (Q3-Q4 2026)
* **Goal:** Transition to a multi-steward environment.
* **Action:** Establish the **501(c)(6)** legal entity to house the T3SI.
* **Action:** Integration of local-first LLM agents (T.E.S.S. reference implementation) for automated manifest auditing.

### 2.3 Horizon 3: The Sovereign Ecosystem (2027+)
* **Goal:** Global adoption of T3S as a "Verifiable Insurability" standard.
* **Action:** Launch of the **T3S Certification Program** for sovereign hardware and software vendors.

## 3.0 Service Portfolio Management
In accordance with ITIL 4, the T3S portfolio consists of:
1.  **Core Standard:** The normative repository and ontologies.
2.  **Reference Implementations:** Tooling (900-block) and Agent Personas (030-block).
3.  **Governance Services:** Certification, auditing, and partnership management.

## 4.0 Stakeholder Engagement (RACI Matrix)
This matrix defines the distribution of authority and labor within the Service Value System to ensure non-repudiation and structural integrity.

| Role | RACI Designation | Functional Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Contributors** | **Responsible (R)** | Technical execution; drafting artifacts; implementing block logic; compliance with 150-series protocols. |
| **Stewards** | **Accountable (A)** | Final approval authority; Markov Blanket integrity; Strategic direction. |
| **Partners** | **Consulted (C)** | 501(c)(6) member organizations providing industry requirements and financial support. |
| **Beneficiaries** | **Informed (I)** | End-users; auditors; third-party systems consuming T3S manifests. |

## 5.0 Continuous Improvement (CSI)
Strategic success is measured by the reduction of "Ontological Drift" and the increase in "Deterministic Velocity"—the speed at which a system can be modified without breaking the Markov Blanket.
