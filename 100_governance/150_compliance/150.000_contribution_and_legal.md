# 150 Legal and Contribution Standard

## 1.0 Purpose (ITIL SVS Alignment)
This standard defines the governance interface for the **Service Value Chain**. It ensures all modifications to the T3S framework are managed as controlled **Changes**, preserving the system's "Verifiable Insurability" and preventing unauthorized "Ontological Drift."

## 2.0 Change Authority and Roles
In alignment with ITIL 4, the following roles are established:
* **Change Authority:** The System Steward (or T3SI Board) holds the authority to approve changes to "Active" artifacts.
* **Service Owner:** The T3SI Initiative, responsible for the end-to-end value delivery of the standard.
* **Contributor:** Any entity submitting a **Change Proposal** (Pull Request/Merge Request).

## 3.0 Mandatory Provenance (The A1 Assertion)
No contribution shall be accepted into the **Sovereign Canon** without satisfying the following forensic requirements:
1.  **DCO Compliance:** All commits must include a `Signed-off-by` line certifying the Developer Certificate of Origin.
2.  **Cryptographic Attestation:** Commits must be signed with a GPG/SSH key registered in the `040_integrity` block to ensure non-repudiation.
3.  **Entity Verification:** Corporate contributors must provide a machine-readable authorization manifest if contributing on behalf of a 501(c)(6) partner.

## 4.0 Governance Gates (ITIL Change Control)
Every contribution must pass through a **Normal Change** workflow:
* **Step 1: Validation.** Automated schema check against `130.000`.
* **Step 2: Risk Assessment.** Peer review for "Markov Blanket Integrity." Does the change introduce external dependencies or data-leakage risks?
* **Step 3: Documentation.** Major changes MUST be accompanied by a **140.xxx ADR** (Architecture Decision Record).
* **Step 4: Authorization.** Final approval and merge by the Change Authority.
