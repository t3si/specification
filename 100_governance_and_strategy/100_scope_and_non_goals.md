# Scope and Non-Goals

## 1.0 Mission Statement
To provide the normative structural foundations and semantic primitives required to eliminate the **Accountability Vacuum** in high-hazard and highly regulated technical environments.

## 2.0 In-Scope (The Stewardship Domain)
The following areas are within the normative authority of T3S:

* **2.1 Ontological Boundary Definition:** The formalization of the **Markov Blanket (000 Block)** to define machine-readable system identity and safety constraints.
* **2.2 Semantic Anchoring:** The mapping of file-system artifacts to global ontologies (ISO, ITIL) via **JSON-LD** to ensure intent fidelity.
* **2.3 The Standard of Care:** Defining technical logic gates that translate regulatory policy into **Verifiable Booleans**.
* **2.4 Provenance & Evidence:** Establishing the **Evidence Database (EDB)** structure within the 500 Block to ensure all system actions are audit-ready and forensic-grade.
* **2.5 Agentic Governance:** Providing the "System Pilot" (T.E.S.S.) and associated persona constraints to ensure AI agents operate as stewards, not autonomous actors outside of the sovereign canon.
* **2.6 Temporal Integrity:** Cryptographic versioning of system intent to ensure that operational evidence is always mapped to the specific governance state at the time of execution.
* **2.7 Human-in-the-Loop Provenance:** Mandatory structures for human-delegated authority, ensuring no automated action occurs without a traceable chain of personhood.

## 3.0 Non-Goals (The Guardrails)
To maintain structural integrity and prevent "Ontological Drift," T3S explicitly excludes the following:

* **3.1 Application Business Logic:** T3S governs the *where* and *why* of data integrity, not the *how* of specific application calculations.
* **3.2 Replacement of Existing Standards:** T3S acts as the Physical Translation Layer for industry standards (e.g., ISO, SOC 2, ITIL, etc.), not a replacement for them. It provides the structural substrate upon which these frameworks are physically implemented.
* **3.3 Real-Time Network Orchestration:** T3S governs configuration, not real-time traffic routing or service mesh logic.
* **3.4 General Purpose CMS:** T3S is for technical governance; it is not a wiki or a junk-drawer for non-taxonomic documentation.
* **3.5 Absolute Autonomy:** T3S forbids systems that can override the **Sovereign Canon** without human provenance.
* **3.6 Ontological Imperialism:** T3S governs the integrity of data within its blanket, but does not seek to define "Truth" for external systems.
* **3.7 Vendor-Specific Lock-in:** T3S must remain executable via local-first, open-source primitives, ensuring **Cloud-Second** sovereignty.
* **3.8 Narrative Ambiguity:** Documents must serve as evidence or specification. Non-linked "fluff" is out of scope.
* **3.9 Real-Time Performance Benchmarking:** T3S is a standard for **integrity and care**, not a tool for general performance or latency tuning.

## 4.0 Governance Gate
Any artifact, automation, or agentic action that falls within the "Non-Goals" domain must be isolated in an external repository or sub-indexed as a non-normative extension.
